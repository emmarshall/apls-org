AP-LS RETAINS ITS APA SEATS; GRAD STUDENT VOTING MEASURE FAILS

Thank you to everyone who voted in the APA Apportionment Ballot this fall. The results determine the composition of APA's Council of Representatives. With the voting now closed, AP-LS retained its two seats for 2021. Two divisions lost a seat (Society for the Psychology of Women; Trauma Psychology) and two divisions gained a seat (Society of Counseling Psychology; Society for the Psychology of Religion and Spirituality). The percentage of votes AP-LS received slightly increased compared to last year, but AP-LS remains very low in the voting tallies. Make sure to vote each year to keep AP-LS' voice heard in APA! In 2020, the AP-LS Council representatives are Jason Cantone and Margaret Bull Kovera.

The bylaws amendment seeking to expand APA voting privileges for graduate students failed by a very narrow margin. Bylaws amendments must be approved by two-thirds (66.67%) of APA voting members, but only 65.98% voted to approve this amendment. The measure passed Council in August 2019 with 73% of Council Representatives voting yes.

LAW AND HUMAN BEHAVIOR UPDATES 

Bradley D. McAuliff, JD, PhD (bradley.mcauliff@csun.edu)

Editor-in-Chief

I am excited to announce that Dr. Jennifer Cox has joined the Editorial Team of Law and Human Behavior. She and Dr. Dave DeMatteo will work with reviewers to make publication decisions for clinical submissions to the journal.

Dr. Cox received her doctorate in clinical psychology from Texas A&M University in 2014. She is currently an Associate Professor who specializes in clinical psychology-law at The University of Alabama. She has served on LHB Editorial Board since 2015 and currently serves on the editorial board of three journals and the AP-LS Committees for Research and Dissertation Awards. Her research interests include the impact of gender and race biases on legal decision making, forensic mental health assessment, psychopathy, and the interaction of psychology and public policy.

Dr. Cox replaces Dr. Amanda Zelechoski, who recently stepped down as Associate Editor to tend to family medical issues and to focus on several new projects. The journal not only benefitted from Dr. Zelechoski’s expertise as AE—she was also the driving force behind the journal’s new Reviewer Mentee Program. On behalf of Division 41, the LHB Editorial Team would like to thank Amanda for her service and we look forward to working with Dr. Cox in her new role as Associate Editor.
