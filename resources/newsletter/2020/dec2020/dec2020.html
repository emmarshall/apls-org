VOTE FOR AP-LS/DIVISION 41!

On November 2, APA members received an e-mailed ballot thatincludes two proposed APA bylaws changes that would promote inclusion in APA governance. In addition, each member is given 10 votes to apportion across APA divisions and SPTAs. This vote determines how many seats AP-LS receives on APA's Council of Representatives. We currently have two but could lose or gain a seat depending on the outcome of this vote. Please give all 10 of your votes to AP-LS/Division 41 so we can keep our AP-LS voices heard! The election is open throughDecember 12.

We are happy to announce the recent election of two AP-LS members toAPA Boards and Committees.Dr. Apryl Alexanderwas elected to the APA Board for the Advancement of Psychology in the Public Interest andDr. Michael Hendrickswasre-elected to the APA Finance Committee. We strongly encourage AP-LS members to seek positions on APA Boards and Committees. If you want to learn more about that process, contact AP-LS Council Representative Jason Cantone (jasoncantone@gmail.com). Open positions are generally announced in late Spring and slates vary each year.

AP-LS NOW PART OF APA’S DIVISIONS FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE

AP-LS applied to join APA’s Divisions for Social Justice (DSJ), and are thrilled to announce that our application was accepted. AP-LS will join 20 other APA Divisions dedicated to social justice. DSJ activities include: Support for ongoing social justice related research, action, and public policy, inclusive of work related to diversity, equity, and human rights; Support to other DSJ divisions on social justice related issues, proposals, and resolutions developed by that division; Convention programming at APA and distributing a list of social-justice oriented symposia sponsored by DSJ divisions at each year’s APA convention; Promoting the appointment of social-justice oriented individuals to APA Committees (through sharing nominations); and Supporting APA’s Public Interest Directorate and its programs. As so much of AP-LS members’ research and practice surrounds issues of social justice, it is most befitting that we join this hard-working group and long overdue.

AP-LS is equally thrilled to announce that Dr. Apryl Alexander has agreed to represent our division in DSJ for a two-year term. Dr. Alexander is an Associate Professor in the Graduate School of Professional Psychology (GSPP) and the Denver Forensic Institute for Research, Service, and Training (Denver FIRST) at the University of Denver. She is a scholar-activist and public impact scholar whohas been interviewed this year by numerous media outlets, includingThe New York Times,The Wallstreet Journal,USA Today, andNBCNightly News, about her research and advocacy work.In addition to these qualities, she is the ideal person to represent AP-LS as Dr. Alexander was also just elected to the APA Board for the Advancement of Psychology in the Public Interest. Congratulations (and thanks!) on both of these roles. We look forward to your reports on DSJ activities and the role AP-LS can play.


NEW LAW AND SCIENCE DISSERTATION GRANT PROGRAM (LSDG)

We are pleased to announce that Arizona State University has launched a new Law and Science Dissertation Grant (LSDG) program, funded through the National Science Foundation (SBE #2016661). This competitive program replaces NSF’s successful and longstanding Doctoral Dissertation Research Improvement Grant (DDRIG) program in Law and Science. The award to ASU will provide funding of up to $20,000 apiece to doctoral students in diverse law-and-science disciplines (e.g., anthropology, criminal justice, economics, forensic science, political science, psychology, sociology) to conduct their dissertation research. The next submission deadline is April 30, 2021.

For more information about the LSDG program, please visit (and share) our website:https://newcollege.asu.edu/law-and-science-diss-grant
