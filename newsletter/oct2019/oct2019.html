LEGAL UPDATE: ASSESSING THREATS OF SELF-DIRECTED VIOLENCE AND DUTIES OF CARE WITHIN HIGHER EDUCATION INSTITUTIONS: NGUYEN V. MASSACHUSETTS INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY (2018)

Whereas threat assessment professionals in the United States and Canada may well be aware of case law, other legal standards, and their professional responsibilities regarding assessing and managing risk of violence toward others, fewer may be as familiar with sources of authority regarding self-directed violence threat assessment. It may be reassuring to know that our professional responsibilities related to assessing and managing other- and self-directed violence are somewhat analogous, and the courts have historically, as well as more recently, underscored this view.

Jennifer Groscup

It was an honor to be elected President of AP-LS, and I am very much looking forward to working with all of you in the upcoming year on our organization. APA COMMITTEE ON LEGAL ISSUES (COLI) SEEKS NEW MEMBERS

The APA Committee on Legal Issues (COLI) is one of the most pertinent ways for AP-LS members to become better involved in APA governance and leadership activities. COLI makes recommendations, prepares and issues reports, and educates APA and psychologists about psycho-legal issues and to promote the field of psychology as it interfaces with the law. COLI does not serve as legal counsel to or form an attorney-client relationship with the APA or the Board of Directors. Instead, COLI has a primary responsibility for serving as a “think tank” for the Board of Directors on psycho-legal issues. 

Nominations are sought for one JD/PhD position and two PhD or PsyD positions. The term of service for all positions is Jan. 1, 2020-Dec. 31, 2022. COLI members must also be APA members. Applications should include (1) a brief statement of no more than 300 words detailing qualifications, and (2) a current curriculum vitae. Nominees' materials will be reviewed by COLI and COLI will send their recommendations to the Board of Directors for review.

For more information, please click here.

Applications are due October 8, 2019 to COLInominations@apa.org.

BETH ROM RYMER SCHOLARSHIPS

Up to nine $5,000 scholarships to support training in psychopharmacology programs.  

The Beth N. Rom-Rymer Scholarships provide support to both graduate students and practicing, licensed psychologists to complete their training in psychopharmacology programs, which adhere to the APA model curriculum of didactic training in clinical psychopharmacology. The purpose of the scholarships is to encourage talented psychologists, with recognized leadership skills, and with demonstrated passion for growing the field, to become prescribing psychologists, engaged in providing the highest caliber, comprehensive, integrated care to the most at-risk members of the community. Preference will be given to those applicants who are committed to working with the underserved. Approximately half of the scholarships are intended for predoctoral graduate students and approximately half for practicing licensed psychologists at all career levels.  

For more information, please click here.

The deadline for applications is February 1, 2020.


MISCARRIAGES OF JUSTICE, TENTH ANNUAL ISSUE 2019-2020

Miscarriages of Justice is a special interdisciplinary issue of Albany Law School’s Albany Law Review, published in collaboration with the University at Albany’s School of Criminal Justice. This annual issue is composed of articles that integrate legal scholarship with social science and/or policy perspectives, and it focuses on problems within or related to the criminal justice system. The tenth annual issue anticipates publication in Spring of 2020. The theme for the 2019–2020 issue is: “Justice Delayed is Justice Denied: A Look at the Influence of Exonerations on Post-Conviction Proceedings in our Legal System and Beyond”.

For more information, please click here.

Submissions are due October 31, 2019.
