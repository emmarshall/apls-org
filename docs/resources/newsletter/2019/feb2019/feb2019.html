AP-LS ELECTIONS FOR 2019-2020; NOMINATIONS DUE TODAY

As described in an email you should have received on January 13, 2019, we have an election upcoming in mid-April that will be held to fill the Division 41 positions of President-elect, Council Representative, and Member-at-Large. For more information about these positions, please see: http://www.apadivisions.org/division-41/leadership/executive-committee/index.aspx.  We are seeking nominations, including self-nominations, for these three positions. 

You must be a current AP-LS member to run for these positions, and you must also be an APA member to run for Council Representative.  If you’re interested in being considered for the election slate, please send the following materials to Eve Brank (ebrank2@unl.edu), Chair of the AP-LS Nominations and Awards Committee: 

  • CV
  • Letter(s) in support of the nominee
  • Statement of interest (for self-nominations)

If you are nominating someone for the election slate, please ensure they are willing to run if they are selected to be on the ballot. All nominations are due by Friday, February 1, 2019. All nominations and questions should be submitted to Eve Brank (ebrank2@unl.edu).

AP-LS  REPRESENTATION AT APA

Thank you to everyone who voted in the APA Apportionment ballot. Because of your votes, AP-LS will retain its two representative seats on APA's Council of Representatives in 2020. AP-LS received slightly more votes than last year but still remains on the low-end for divisions having two representatives. Your current representatives are Jason Cantone and Tom Grisso. In this election, three APA divisions lost a seat (Divisions 17 - Counseling; 32 - Humanistic; and 36 - Religion and Spirituality), and three APA divisions gained a seat (Divisions 39 - Psychoanalysis; 42 - Independent Practice; and 45 - Culture, Ethnicity and Race). Your vote matters! APA's Council of Representatives meets again in February 2019.

NOMINATION SOUGHT FOR BOOK SERIES EDITOR

We are seeking applications for our AP-LS Book Series Editor as the term is coming to an end for our current editor, Patricia Zapf. The goal of the book series is to disseminate results of research and scholarly writing in the area of psychology and law.

The Book Series Editor is a non-voting, ex-officio member of the AP-LS Executive Committee and serves within the Publications Committee.  As detailed in our bylaws, “the Editor shall be appointed for a five-year term and shall serve as a non-voting, ex-officio member of the Executive Committee. By mutual consent of the book series Editor and the Executive Committee, the appointment may be extended for an additional five-year term, for a maximum of ten years.”

The official term will begin in August, but we have the goal of naming the appointment in March in order to give sufficient transition time with the new and current editor. You must be a current AP-LS member to be appointed to this position. 

If you are interested in being considered for this position, please send the following materials to Eve Brank (ebrank2@unl.edu), Chair of the AP-LS Nominations and Awards Committee, by Friday, February 22, 2019: 

  • CV
  • Statement of interest

AAFP DISSERTATION GRANTS

The American Academy of Forensic Psychology (AAFP) makes available up to $5000 (maximum award is $1,500 per applicant) for grants to graduate students conducting dissertations in applied areas of law and psychology, with preference shown for dissertations addressing clinical-forensic issues.

For more information go to: http://aafpforensic.org/grants/dissertation-grants/.

Submissions should be e-mailed no later than March 31st to the President-Elect of AAFP, Bob Stinson, Psy.D., J.D., LICDC-CS, ABPP at stinson@stinsonandassociates.com. 


LEGAL UPDATE: THE REAUTHORIZATION OF THE JUVENILE JUSTICE AND DELINQUENCY PREVENTION ACT (2018)

The Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention Act (JJDPA) incentivizes the adoption of particular juvenile justice policies by granting funding to states that meet core requirements. Originally passed in 1974, the JJDPA has been reauthorized with modifications several times since--most recently in December 2018. In this month's Legal Update column, Dr. Emily Haney-Caron and Ms. Erika Diaz Ortiz discuss the implications of the recent reauthorization for clinicians, forensic evaluators, and researchers.
