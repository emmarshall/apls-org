Welcome to the December 2019 

American Psychology-Law Society (AP-LS) Newsletter.


Our monthly Newsletter provides information about activities, upcoming events, and resources to connect the psychology-law community.
 
AP-LS aspires to excel as a valuable, effective, and influential organization advancing the science of psychology-law and the translation of psychology-law knowledge into practice and policy.

SEEKING VOLUNTEERS FOR OUTREACH EFFORTS IN NEW ORLEANS

As some of you may remember, we did outreach efforts in Memphis and Portland at our 2018 and 2019 conferences. This year, we plan to do similar outreach efforts in New Orleans middle and high schools. Therefore, we’re asking you to fill out a very simple form online to indicate your interest. We will use those responses to create our list of potential volunteers and then we will be in touch in February to work out the details. Please follow this link by Wednesday, January 1, 2020.

As we did in years past, we will be going into local schools on Thursday morning (March 5th) before AP-LS officially begins and talking with students about law-psychology generally and the specific research interests of our members. We got overwhelmingly positive feedback from the local students and AP-LS members from our previous experiences so we’re looking forward to another great experience this year.

AP-LS BOOK AWARD COMMITTEE INVITES NOMINATIONS

The AP-LS Book Award Committee invites nominations to recognize outstanding scholarship in psychology and law edited books published in 2017 and 2018. The deadline for nominations has been extended to December 6, 2019.  Nominations (including self-nominations) and e-versions of the book should be sent to the Chair of the Committee:  Marie Comiskey, BA LLB LLM SJD, Marie.Comiskey@utoronto.ca. 

The award will be presented at the upcoming AP-LS Conference in Portland in March 2020.  The award rotates annually between edited and authored books.

DISSERTATION AWARDS

The American Psychology-Law Society confers Dissertation Awards for scientific research and scholarship relevant to the promotion of the interdisciplinary study of psychology and law. Students who complete dissertations involving basic or applied research in psychology and law in 2019 are encouraged to apply for these awards. First, second, and third-place awards will be conferred; winners will be invited to present their research at the annual AP-LS conference and receive a cash award. Submissions are due by 12/31/19.

For application details please click here, or, for more information, please contact Jacqueline Evans, the chair of the Dissertation Awards Committee, at jacevans@fiu.edu.
