CALL FOR PAPERS: BEHAVIORAL SCIENCES AND THE LAW – SPECIAL ISSUE ON POLICING

Behavioral Sciences & the Law announces a forthcoming special issue on Policing, to be edited by Christopher Slobogin, J.D., LL.M., a senior editor for the journal. Manuscripts that address the following issues are especially welcome: (1) research on the “benefits” and “costs” of various policing techniques, measured in procedural justice or other metrics; (2) research on how police should handle people with mental illness; (3) research on the psychological effects of surveillance by the police; (4) research on the psychological impact of policing on police officers.

EXPERT OPINION: UPCOMING THREE PART SERIES

Best practices tell us to choose empirically supported treatment for the individual client. Though we have successfully developed such treatments for many forensic populations, there remains a lack of clearly identified and supported evidenced-based practices for individuals who have committed sexual offenses. In this three-part series, Amanda M. Fanniff, Ph.D., Associate Professor at Palo Alto University, will review current practices and examine whether such treatments actually “work.” This month’s column, Does Treatment for Adults who have Committed Sexual Offenses Work? A Simple Question with a Complicated Answer, focuses on adults who have committed sexual offenses and the next two will look at adolescents who have committed sexual offenses as well as minor attracted persons who want to remain offense-free. We start by asking the question, “How do we measure treatment in this area?”.

AP-LS BOOK AWARD COMMITTEE INVITES NOMINATIONS

The AP-LS Book Award Committee invites nominations to recognize outstanding scholarship in psychology and law edited books published in 2017 and 2018. The deadline for nominations is November 8, 2019.  Nominations (including self-nominations) and e-versions of the book should be sent to the Chair of the Committee:  Marie Comiskey, BA LLB LLM SJD, Marie.Comiskey@utoronto.ca. The award will be presented at the upcoming AP-LS Conference in Portland in March 2020. The award rotates annually between edited and authored books.
