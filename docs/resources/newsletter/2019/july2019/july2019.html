Our monthly Newsletter provides information about activities, upcoming events, and resources to connect the psychology-law community.
 
AP-LS aspires to excel as a valuable, effective, and influential organization advancing the science of psychology-law and the translation of psychology-law knowledge into practice and policy.  
 

TEACHING IN PSYCHOLOGY AND LAW: BRINGING THE CRIMINAL JUSTICE SYSTEM TO LIFE INSIDE THE CLASSROOM

Content Contributor: Sara Appleby, Ph.D.

When a last minute cancellation put her prison-based course back in the traditional classroom, Dr. Appleby had to quickly find ways to bring the complex dynamics and real voices of the criminal justice system to life for her students.  This month, she shares diverse resources and methods for use inside the classroom.

CALL FOR PAPERS: BEHAVIORAL SCIENCES AND THE LAW – SPECIAL ISSUE ON POLICING

Behavioral Sciences & the Law announces a forthcoming special issue on Policing, to be edited by Christopher Slobogin, J.D., LL.M., a senior editor for the journal. Manuscripts that address the following issues are especially welcome: (1) research on the “benefits” and “costs” of various policing techniques, measured in procedural justice or other metrics; (2) research on how police should handle people with mental illness; (3) research on the psychological effects of surveillance by the police; (4) research on the psychological impact of policing on police officers.

SPECIAL JOURNAL ISSUE ON JUVENILE JUSTICE RESEARCH

Described by:  Thomas Grisso, Erika Fountain, Amanda NeMoyer & Laura Thornton

The June 2019 e issue of the APA journal Translational Issues in Psychological Science is a special issue  titled “Psychological Science and Juvenile Justice.”  Its  editorial classifies the various phases or steps in the research process of translating basic behavioral science to create, implement and evaluate methods for use in juvenile justice. The eight peer-reviewed  articles in the special issue demonstrate various levels of translational research. To read this column, click here.

AP-LS VOICES HEARD IN APA

Congratulations to Dr. Erika Fountain on her selection as a member of the new APA Advisory Group for Applied Psychology. The advisory group, which will be led by APA's Office of Applied Psychology, will have two main charges: 1) to make recommendations about how applied psychology can be better represented in APA; and 2) serve as a source of input and feedback regarding applied psychology issues and priorities. We are excited to see AP-LS part of this important advisory group.

Jason Cantone was selected for the new APA Work Group on Accommodating Young Families. The work group is tasked with examining how APA could better support young families attending governance meetings and the annual convention (e.g., child care grants, on-site child care, nursing resources, family-friendly resources). During the first meeting, AP-LS was commended for its child care grants to assist families attending the annual AP-LS conference. 

If you're interested in serving on an APA group, APA is currently looking for new members for its Continuing Education Committee (applications due August 12), Committee on Early Career Psychologists (due August 28), Committee on Disability Issues in Psychology (due August 30), and APA Fellows Committee (due September 10). Contact APA Council Representative Jason Cantone (jasoncantone@gmail.com) for application materials.
