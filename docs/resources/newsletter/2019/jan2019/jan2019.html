EXPERT OPINION: A SPOT ON A COW

Content Contributor: Antoinette Kavanaugh, Ph.D., ABPP

At a recent family reunion in North Carolina, I was having breakfast with my aunt and her family. At one point, her kids shared a story about while growing up, they would go to events like the state fair and be one of the few families of color present. They would look at each other, smile, and say “Spot on a cow.” This is how they acknowledged that they were some of the few people of color at an event or outing. In this brief essay I will explain how being a spot on a cow led to my becoming a forensic psychologist and how it continues to shape the way I conceptualize and conduct forensic interviews.

AP-LS CE COMMITTEE ANNOUNCEMENTS

The AP-LS Continuing Education (CE) Committee is pleased to offer 10 grants to students and early career psychologists (defined as 7 years or less since last terminal degree was awarded) to attend pre-conference CE workshops on March 13, 2019. To apply for one of these grants (which cover the workshop registration fee), please complete the application at this link by Friday, January 11, 2019.  Grant recipients will be notified by January 25, 2018 (prior to the early registration rate deadline; Note: if you have already registered for a workshop, you can still apply and receive a refund for your registration fee). 

The Oregon State Bar (OSB) has approved the workshops for general minimum continuing legal education credits. OSB members can receive up to 6.5 hours of credit for a full-day session and 3.25 hours of credit for a half-day session.

Descriptions of the workshops can be found here. Register for workshops here.

LHB IS GOING GREEN IN 2019

In August 2017, the Division 41 Executive Committee unanimously voted to renew its lucrative contract with the American Psychological Association to publish Law and Human Behavior through 2023. One important change in the new contract is that AP-LS members will no longer receive a complimentary print subscription to the journal beginning with the first issue of Volume 43 in February 2019.

To access current and past issues of LHB, AP-LS members should log into MyAPA using the email address they have on file with the division. All AP-LS members have a MyAPA account even if they are not members of the American Psychological Association.

AP-LS members who wish to continue receiving a print subscription of LHB should contact APA directly. Details and prices can be found here under the Subscriptions Individuals button.
